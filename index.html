Abstract

This paper examines the complementary use of traditional palaeographic methods and computational approaches in the identification and analysis of scribal hands. Taking as a case study the medieval scribes Raoulet d'Orléans and Henri de Trévou, it investigates how digitally assisted hand analysis can corroborate, refine, or challenge attributions made by earlier scholarship. The study presents a methodological framework for integrating expert knowledge with quantitative techniques, contributing to ongoing debates about the role of digital tools in historical linguistics and manuscript studies.
